# Task 05 – Percentage Calculation

## Problem Statement

A measurement of time is recorded as

$$
t = 5.45 \pm 0.22 \ \text{seconds}
$$

What is the percentage uncertainty of this measurement?

## Theory

Percentage uncertainty is calculated as:

$$
\text{Percentage uncertainty} = \frac{\Delta t}{|t|} \times 100\%
$$

## Step-by-Step Solution

**Step 1: Identify values**

Nominal value: $t = 5.45 \ \text{s}$

Absolute uncertainty: $\Delta t = 0.22 \ \text{s}$

**Step 2: Calculate relative uncertainty**

$$
\frac{\Delta t}{t} = \frac{0.22}{5.45}
$$

$$
0.22 \div 5.45 = 0.04036697
$$

**Step 3: Convert to percentage**

$$
\text{Percentage uncertainty} = 0.04036697 \times 100\% = 4.0367\%
$$

Round to two significant figures (matching $\Delta t$):

$$
\text{Percentage uncertainty} \approx 4.0\%
$$

## Final Result

$$
\text{Percentage uncertainty} = 4.0\%
$$

## Interpretation

The measurement has a relative uncertainty of about 4%. This means the absolute uncertainty ($0.22$ s) is about 4% of the measured value ($5.45$ s). Percentage uncertainty is useful for comparing precision across different measurements regardless of scale.
