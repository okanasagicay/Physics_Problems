# Task 05 – Parallel Plate Capacitor Analysis

## Problem Statement

Given $S = 0.02 \, \text{m}^2$, $d = 5 \, \text{mm}$, $U = 500 \, \text{V}$.

## Theory

$$
C = \varepsilon_0 \frac{S}{d}
$$

$$
U = \frac{1}{2} C V^2
$$

$$
E = \frac{V}{d}
$$

$$
F = \frac{1}{2} \varepsilon_0 S E^2
$$

## Step-by-Step Solution

$$
C = 8.854 \times 10^{-12} \frac{0.02}{0.005} = 3.54 \times 10^{-11} \, \text{F}
$$

$$
U = \frac{1}{2} (3.54 \times 10^{-11})(500)^2 = 4.43 \times 10^{-6} \, \text{J}
$$

$$
E = \frac{500}{0.005} = 1 \times 10^5 \, \text{V/m}
$$

$$
F = \frac{1}{2} (8.854 \times 10^{-12})(0.02)(10^5)^2 = 8.85 \times 10^{-4} \, \text{N}
$$

## Final Result

All values calculated above.

## Interpretation

Stronger electric fields increase both stored energy and plate attraction.
