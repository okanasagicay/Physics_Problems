# Task 10 – Speed of Light in Diamond

## Problem Statement

The refractive index of diamond is $n = 2.42$.  
Determine the speed of light inside the diamond.

## Theory

The refractive index of a medium is defined as the ratio of the speed of light in vacuum to the speed of light in that medium:

$$
n = \frac{c}{v}
$$

where

- $c = 3.00 \times 10^8 \, \text{m/s}$ is the speed of light in vacuum,
- $v$ is the speed of light in the material.

Rearranging the formula to solve for $v$:

$$
v = \frac{c}{n}
$$

This equation shows that the larger the refractive index, the slower light travels in that material.

## Step-by-Step Solution

Substitute the known values into the equation.

$$
v = \frac{3.00 \times 10^8}{2.42}
$$

Performing the division:

$$
v = 1.24 \times 10^8 \, \text{m/s}
$$

## Final Result

$$
v = 1.24 \times 10^8 \, \text{m/s}
$$

## Interpretation

Light travels significantly slower in diamond than in vacuum.  
This is because diamond has a very high refractive index, meaning the electromagnetic wave interacts strongly with the atoms of the material, which reduces its propagation speed.
