# Task 04 – Magnetic Torque on a Current Loop

## Problem Statement

A rectangular loop $10 \, \text{cm} \times 5 \, \text{cm}$ carries $2 \, \text{A}$. A magnetic field $B = 0.3 \, \text{T}$ is parallel to the plane of the loop.

## Theory

Torque on a loop:

$$
\tau = I A B \sin\theta
$$

Here $\theta = 90^\circ$.

## Step-by-Step Solution

Area:

$$
A = 0.10 \times 0.05 = 0.005 \, \text{m}^2
$$

$$
\tau = 2 \times 0.005 \times 0.3
$$

$$
\tau = 3 \times 10^{-3} \, \text{N}\cdot\text{m}
$$

## Final Result

$$
\tau = 3 \times 10^{-3} \, \text{N}\cdot\text{m}
$$

## Interpretation

Maximum torque occurs when the field is parallel to the loop plane.
