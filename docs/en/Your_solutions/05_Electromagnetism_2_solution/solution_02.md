# Task 02 – Ampere's Law and Magnetic Field Between Wires

## Problem Statement

Two long parallel wires are $10 \, \text{cm}$ apart and carry currents of $5 \, \text{A}$ in opposite directions. Find the magnetic field at the midpoint.

## Theory

The magnetic field due to a long straight wire is

$$
B = \frac{\mu_0 I}{2 \pi r}
$$

where $\mu_0 = 4\pi \times 10^{-7} \, \text{T}\cdot\text{m/A}$.

## Step-by-Step Solution

Distance from midpoint to each wire:

$$
r = 0.05 \, \text{m}
$$

Magnetic field from one wire:

$$
B_1 = \frac{4\pi \times 10^{-7} \times 5}{2\pi \times 0.05}
$$

$$
B_1 = 2 \times 10^{-5} \, \text{T}
$$

Since currents are opposite, fields at midpoint are in the same direction and add:

$$
B_{\text{total}} = 2 B_1
$$

$$
B_{\text{total}} = 4 \times 10^{-5} \, \text{T}
$$

## Final Result

$$
B = 4 \times 10^{-5} \, \text{T}
$$

## Interpretation

Opposite currents cause magnetic fields to reinforce between the wires.
