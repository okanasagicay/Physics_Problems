# Task 01 – Gauss's Law and Electric Flux

## Problem Statement

A point charge of $+2 \, \text{C}$ is located at the origin. Calculate the electric flux through a spherical surface of radius $1 \, \text{m}$ centered at the origin.

## Theory

Gauss's Law relates the electric flux through a closed surface to the charge enclosed by that surface:

$$
\Phi_E = \frac{Q_{\text{enc}}}{\varepsilon_0}
$$

where $\varepsilon_0 = 8.854 \times 10^{-12} \, \text{C}^2/(\text{N}\cdot\text{m}^2)$.

## Step-by-Step Solution

The spherical surface encloses the point charge completely. Therefore,

$$
Q_{\text{enc}} = 2 \, \text{C}
$$

Applying Gauss's Law,

$$
\Phi_E = \frac{2}{8.854 \times 10^{-12}}
$$

$$
\Phi_E = 2.26 \times 10^{11} \, \text{N}\cdot\text{m}^2/\text{C}
$$

## Final Result

$$
\Phi_E = 2.26 \times 10^{11} \, \text{N}\cdot\text{m}^2/\text{C}
$$

## Interpretation

The electric flux depends only on the enclosed charge and not on the radius of the spherical surface.
