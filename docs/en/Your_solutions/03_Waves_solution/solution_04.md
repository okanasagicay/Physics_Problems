Question:
What is the phase difference in radians between two points on a wave that are separated by a distance of λ/3?

Step 1 – Recall the phase difference formula
For a wave, the phase difference Δφ between two points separated by a distance Δx is:

Δϕ=k⋅Δx
where k is the wave number:

k=2π/λ

​Step 2 – Insert the given separation
The problem says:

Δx=λ/3

Substitute into the formula:

Δϕ=2π/λ . λ/3

​Step 3 – Simplify

Cancel λ: Δϕ=2π/3

Step 4 – Final answer
2π/3 radians
This is approximately 2.094 radians,or120∘.

Step 5 – Interpretation
A phase difference of 2π/3 means the two points are not in phase.
One point reaches its maximum displacement 120∘ (1/3 of a cycle) after the other.
